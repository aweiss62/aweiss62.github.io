Microwaves & Fridges


Summary:

  • Date & Naming System for Fridges
  • Weekly Cleansing of the Fridges
  • Wax Paper for Microwaving
  • Get Facilities Involved
    • ---------------------------------------------

    You have spoken and I have listened. While I went and cleaned the microwaves this morning, a one-time effort isn’t going to fix the problem that the kitchens at GW are gross. As a result, my team and I have come up with a plan to begin mitigating the problem.

    Dates & Names – First, after notifying the student body and posting signs on all the refrigerators, the SBA would begin providing stickers and a working sharpie at every fridge. Upon using the fridge, any person to do so would be consenting to the ‘Fridge Agreement.’ Yes we are all lawyers here. This agreement would simply require that prior to using the fridge, the user would have to write their name and the current date on their food. This would allow for the proper disposal of expired, or forgotten food. Real estate is valuable enough in those things without abandoned food taking up the shelves.

    Weekly Cleansing - The second phase of the plan, would be to start enforcing the Fridge Agreement. This would occur by assigning the VP of Student Affairs (or someone to which they delegate), the task of cleansing the fridges every Monday of food from the previous week.

    Wax Paper - An easy but effective solution that would stem some of the mess, would be for the SBA to provide Wax Paper Sheets at each microwave to cover food. This would cost at most $2970/yr, assuming the worst (see below). We have the budget and it would go a long way.

    Facilities - The final step would be getting the Administration to take it over (specifically Dean Molinengo). While we could do it within the parameters of the SBA, the goal would be to transition it to the facilities department. If such a discussion were to go beyond my term in office, I would leave detailed instructions on how to continue such negotiations.

    Hopefully, this would at least begin to address the microwaves and fridges.
