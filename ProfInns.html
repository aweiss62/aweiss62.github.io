Inns of Court Reform


Summary:

  • Work with Prof. Peterson to refine the Focus
  • More Application
    • ---------------------------------------------

    GW’s innovative skills program has been recognized throughout the field as a creative solution to the long time problem of real world skills being taught to Law Students. Despite this however, it has been recognized that as the program has stumbled with its identity throughout its infancy, there are many areas in which the program can improve.

    Simply put, the inns-of-court program tries to do too much. Through numerous conversations, students agree that while the lessons are valuable, some are worth more than others. Instead of meditation, let’s learn join with the career center and critique each other’s resumes instead of example ones. Instead of talking about how valuable academic advising, let’s do it. Ensure we know how to apply on simplicity via an interactive tutorial instead of pointing to a website. The inns-of-court program is a phenomenal idea, but like any inaugural venture, it also has its kinks to work out.

    Currently, the program is far too broad and touchy feely. Law students worry and are stressed because they’re lost in the torrent of 1L year. As President, I would use the regular meetings with Prof. Peterson to convey that instead of telling us not to worry about being lost, they should give us a map.
