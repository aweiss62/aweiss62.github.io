Mental Health


Summary:

  • Continued Priority in the Dean of Students Search
  • In-House Counselor AT the Law School
  • Manditory Mentor Training
    • ---------------------------------------------

    Mental Health is easily one of my top priorities. Graduate school is already a stressful enough scenario without the watchful eye of the curve over your shoulder. When you add in however, the perfectionist atmosphere of our classes, the competitive nature of the Boards, and then the pressure of Journal, it’s a lot for anyone. With that, GW Law has a need to do more for mental health than just tell us how to relax and throw events. There’s the need for some change and help.

    Already at work, SBA has been avidly tackling this important issue through various mediums, including increasing the profile of mental health to a top priority as we search for a new Dean of Students, to avidly lobbying the GW Counseling center to provide us NOT ONLY with a Law School-specific Counselor, but one in-house, at the Law School. Additionally, when Shelby (current VP Outreach), Jesse (Chief of Staff), and I all went to Chicago to represent GW Law at the Annual ABA Conference, we attended numerous conferences on Mental Health and how the current game is changing.

    As President, I would start by implementing a Mental Health training for the entire SBA Mentoring program. This would ensure that any mentee who was having trouble, whether with adjusting to the law school rigors, or just stuff at home, would be able to reach out to a friend. Additionally, it would have the added benefit of teaching the Mentors themselves what the resources of the school were and that it’s okay to talk about mental health. Few people even know that we get 6 free appointments each semester at the counseling center in the Marvin Center. We need to spread the word.

    Mental Health is a serious issue, and one that should not be taken lightly. Many times those who need help are the ones we least suspect. By continuing the battle for the resources above, and implementing the mentor training program, my hope is that we can start a new trend of combatting this issue as a community and bring the discussion back into the light.
