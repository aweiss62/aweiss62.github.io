Transfer Inn of Court


Summary:

  • Transfer Students have Unique Challenges
  • Establish a Transfer Inn of Court
    • ---------------------------------------------

    A specific recommendation to the inns-of-court program would be the creation of a Transfer Inn. While I understand the desire to have transfer students integrate with the school, and become part of their Judicial Families, the fact of the matter is that they don’t. By 2L, the inns have already established their social structures and no longer share classes (the critical forum of interaction which established this brotherhood). Additionally, it’s not like we don’t have the numbers; 97 Transfer Students entered in 2014, so already that group is larger than two other inns. Furthermore, if any particular group could benefit from cohesion, brotherhood, and counsel, it’s the 2L transfer students.

    A transfer inn would introduce the transfer students to other students going through the same challenges of integration, academic transition, and organizational inundation. Together they could pool their knowledge and form support networks; support networks many transfer students complain they are lacking here.

    Finally, by pooling this group into a concentrated inn, the administration and SBA would have access to a forum in which we could coordinate solutions to specific issues that only effect Transfer Students. Issues like the above listed regarding academic advising. Situations like a lack of study groups, or a misunderstanding of class registration. These issues, while mundane, are important, especially as you start a new life and want to get off on the right foot.
